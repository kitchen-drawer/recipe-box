Arroz a la Tumbada (Mexican Seafood Rice)

This is a hearty, rustic, and incredibly flavorful "laid-down" rice, meaning it finishes cooking off the heat, allowing all the flavors to meld beautifully. It's truly a celebration of the sea, even with readily available pantry staples. If you start prepping about 45 minutes to an hour before you want to eat, you'll be sitting down to a steaming bowl of deliciousness right on time for dinner!

Basics

  • Prep time: 15 minutes
  • Cook time: 30-35 minutes
  • Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 1 small white onion, sliced about ½-inch thick
  • 3 garlic cloves, unpeeled
  • 1-2 fresh jalapeño chiles, halved lengthwise
  • One 15-ounce can fire-roasted diced tomatoes, undrained
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 cup long-grain white rice
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 6 ounces firm white fish, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • One 6.5-ounce can chopped clams, undrained
  • One 6.5-ounce can crab meat, drained and picked over
  • 12 medium-large (about 9 ounces) cooked shrimp, frozen
  • About ½ cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • Salt, to taste
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges

Method

  1. Prep Your Aromatics (The Heart of the Flavor): Preheat your broiler. Arrange your onion slices, the unpeeled garlic cloves, and your halved jalapeño chiles on a baking sheet. Slide this under the hot broiler, about 4 inches from the element. Roast them for about 5 to 6 minutes, until they are blistered and blackened in spots. Flip them over and let the other side get equally toasty.
  2. Make the Tomato Seasoning: Carefully scrape the roasted onion, garlic, and chiles into a food processor or blender. Add the entire can of fire-roasted diced tomatoes, juice and all. Process this mixture until it's a coarse purée.
  3. Build the Base in the Pot: Pour in the olive oil into a large (at least 4-quart) saucepan or Dutch oven and set it over medium-high heat. Once the oil shimmers, pour in your prepared tomato seasoning. Stir constantly, scraping the bottom of the pan, until the mixture thickens and becomes glossy, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add the Rice and Broth: Add the rice to the pot and stir it into the tomato mixture for about a minute, coating each grain. Now, pour in the chicken broth. Bring the mixture to a rolling boil, then immediately reduce the heat to a gentle simmer.
  5. Introduce the Seafood (Staggered for Perfection): Gently add your fresh fish pieces to the simmering broth. Add the canned chopped clams along with their liquid. Cover the pot and set a timer for 15 minutes. While this cooks, quickly thaw your frozen cooked shrimp by running them under cold water for a minute or two, then drain them well.
  6. The "Tumbada" Moment and Final Touches: When your timer goes off, lift the lid and taste a kernel of rice. If it's still a bit too firm, re-cover the pot and cook for another 2 to 3 minutes. Once the rice is almost done, gently stir in your thawed cooked shrimp and the drained canned crab meat. Re-cover the pot, then turn off the heat entirely. Let the dish rest, or "tumbada," for 2 to 3 minutes.
  7. Season and Serve: Finally, stir in the fresh cilantro. Taste the arroz a la tumbada and add salt to your liking. Ladle generous portions into warm bowls and serve immediately with fresh lime wedges for each person to squeeze over their portion.

Notes

  • Rice Texture: The beauty of this dish is its soupy consistency. Don't expect a dry, fluffy rice like a pilaf. It's meant to be comforting and saucy.
  • Adjusting Heat: The amount of jalapeño can be adjusted to your preference. If you like more heat, add another chile or don't remove the seeds from the ones you use.
  • Seafood Substitutions: This recipe is incredibly forgiving! If you have other cooked seafood on hand, like scallops or even leftover cooked lobster, feel free to add it in step 6 with the shrimp and crab.
