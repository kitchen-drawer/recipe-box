One-Pan Orzo With Spinach and Feta

Think of this dish as a riff on spanakorizo, the classic Greek spinach and rice dish, but here we’re using orzo, which makes for a creamy, satisfying texture without ever feeling heavy. We'll start by building our flavor foundation, then simmer the orzo right in the pan with the vegetables, and finish it with some bright, fresh ingredients. The beauty of a one-pan dish is that you're building layer upon layer of flavor, and you don't even have to wash a separate pot for the pasta. Just give yourself about 30 minutes from start to finish, and this is the perfect weeknight meal.

Basics

  • Prep time: 10 minutes
  • Cook time: 20 minutes
  • Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 4 large scallions, trimmed and thinly sliced
  • 2 large garlic cloves, minced
  • 8 ounces baby spinach leaves (8 cups), coarsely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 3/4 cups low-sodium chicken or vegetable stock
  • 1 cup orzo
  • 1 teaspoon finely grated lemon zest (from 1 lemon)
  • 3/4 cup crumbled feta (3 ounces), plus more for garnish
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas, thawed (optional)
  • 1 cup chopped fresh dill, or use parsley or cilantro

Method

  1. Set a 10-inch skillet over medium heat. Drop in the butter and let it melt and get foamy, about 30 seconds to a minute. Add about three-quarters of your sliced scallions, along with all the minced garlic. Keep the rest of the scallions aside for garnish. Sauté, stirring frequently, until the aromatics have softened and you can really smell them, about 3 minutes.
  2. Add your chopped spinach to the pan. If it doesn't all fit at once, just toss in a handful at a time and let it wilt down before adding more. Sprinkle in ½ teaspoon of salt. Continue cooking and stirring until all the spinach has collapsed and is a deep, glistening green, about 5 minutes.
  3. Pour in the stock and bring it to a gentle simmer. Add the orzo, the lemon zest, and the remaining ½ teaspoon of salt. Give it a good stir, cover the pan, and turn the heat down to medium-low. Let it simmer for 10 to 14 minutes, or until the orzo is almost cooked through and most of the liquid has been absorbed, stirring once or twice to make sure nothing sticks. The grains should have a subtle bite but not be crunchy.
  4. Uncover the pan. Gently stir in the crumbled feta, the thawed peas (if you’re using them), and the chopped dill. Cover the pan again and let it cook for just one more minute. This will finish cooking the orzo, warm the peas, and allow the cheese and herbs to melt into the dish, making the sauce creamy and tangy.
  5. To serve, spoon the orzo into bowls. Sprinkle with the reserved scallions and some more crumbled feta for a salty finish.
