Chilled Sesame Noodles

This recipe focuses on building a deeply flavorful, emulsified sesame sauce and perfectly cooked, chilled noodles that retain their texture. It’s a creamy, velvety, and flavorful experience with a kick from chili oil and raw scallions, and a deep, comforting toasted sesame flavor. Plan to start prepping and cooking about 30-45 minutes before you plan to serve.

Ingredients

For the Sauce:

  • 100% sesame paste 1/2 cup (about 120g)
  • Sesame oil 2 tablespoons
  • Fermented tofu (furu), mashed 1 small cube (about 10g)
  • Light soy sauce 2 tablespoons
  • Dark soy sauce (for color) 1 teaspoon
  • Salt 1/2 teaspoon, or to taste
  • Granulated sugar 1 tablespoon, or to taste
  • Aged Chinese black vinegar 1 tablespoon
  • Sichuan peppercorn oil 1 teaspoon, or to taste
  • Kewpie mayonnaise 2 tablespoons
  • Cold water 1/2 cup, or as needed

For the Noodles:

  • Flour noodles (such as fresh ramen or dried wheat noodles) 1 pound
  • Vegetable oil 1 tablespoon

For the Garnish:

  • Scallions, thinly sliced into ribbons 3-4
  • Chili oil 2-3 tablespoons, or to taste
  • Toasted sesame seeds, freshly ground 1 tablespoon

Equipment

  • Large mixing bowl
  • Whisk
  • Large pot for boiling noodles
  • Fine-mesh sieve or colander
  • Baking sheet or large plate

Instructions

  1. Prepare your mise en place:

    Before you even think about cooking, let's get everything ready. Slice your scallions into beautiful ribbons. If you have a mortar and pestle, give those toasted sesame seeds a quick grind; this really unlocks their flavor. Measure out all your sauce ingredients. This little bit of foresight will make the whole process flow like a dream.

  2. Craft the Sesame Sauce:

    In a large mixing bowl, combine the sesame paste, sesame oil, mashed fermented tofu, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, salt, sugar, aged Chinese black vinegar, Sichuan peppercorn oil, and Kewpie mayonnaise. Now, here's where the magic happens: whisk everything together vigorously. You'll notice it's quite thick at first. Gradually, and I mean gradually, add the cold water, whisking continuously. You're looking for a smooth, velvety emulsion, like a luxurious, pourable cream. Keep whisking until it's perfectly smooth and has that beautiful, rich consistency. Taste it! This is your moment to adjust for salt, sweetness, or a little more tang from the vinegar. Remember, you're the chef here, and your palate is the ultimate guide.

  3. Cook the Noodles with Finesse:

    Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil. Before dropping your noodles in, gently separate them to prevent clumping. Cook them according to the package instructions until they are perfectly al dente—tender but with a slight bite. Once cooked, drain them thoroughly. Now, here's a crucial tip from Lucas Sin himself: skip the ice bath! While it seems counterintuitive for chilled noodles, an ice bath can actually strip away their texture and flavor. Instead, toss the hot, drained noodles with a tablespoon of vegetable oil. This coats them lightly, preventing them from sticking together as they cool and helping them retain their lovely texture. Spread them out on a baking sheet or a large plate to cool down quickly to room temperature.

  4. Assemble and Garnish:

    Once your noodles are cool, transfer them to a serving bowl. Pour a generous amount of that glorious sesame sauce over the noodles. Toss gently to coat every strand evenly. Now for the garnishes: scatter those beautiful scallion ribbons over the top, drizzle with your favorite chili oil for a touch of heat, and finish with a sprinkle of your freshly ground toasted sesame seeds.

  5. Serve and Savor:

    Plate your chilled sesame noodles with pride. Take a moment to admire the creamy coating, the vibrant green of the scallions, and the rich red of the chili oil. This dish is meant to be enjoyed immediately, allowing you to experience the full spectrum of its creamy, velvety texture, the delightful heat from the chili oil, the fresh bite of raw scallions, and that deep, comforting toasted sesame flavor.
